About The Selector:


Selectors main mission is to provide a basic, simple, and fun support to help you at the final stage of your decision-making process.
It doesn't aim to remove your judgment, but it`s purpose is to offer an informed recommendation based on your values.
Comparison is limited to a minimum of 2 options, but 3 would be more recommended as it will offer a wider area of selection but still enough to take an informed decision. If you have more than 3 options, then probably you have at least one to many and you should aim to further reduce your number of choices.
Enjoy!

HOW TO USE
×

How to use the selector:


1. Start by defining your criteria and write them down in the first column. We have decided on 5 as we believe it will determine you as the user to think hard about what is important for you. Use a very brief(ideally 1 word) description.
Example : color, size, composition, etc.
2. Select from 1-5 how important the criteria is for you by using the drop-down option, 1 being lowest importance and 5 highest. Think well and try to differentiate as much as possible between how important each criteria is.
3. Click in each “Click to add option” field under the 3 options(Option A, B, C) and write a short name for your option. Try to limit to a very short name, ideally limit yourself to 1 word.
4. Using the drop down menu under each option select a score from 1 to 10 where 1 is lowest score and 10 is highest.

NOTE: All drop-down boxes in both the Criteria Importance area and in the Option scoring area need to be filled in. Option C scoring remains optional for users with only 2 options.
